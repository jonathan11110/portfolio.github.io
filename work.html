Introduction

LifeGuard

I first became a lifeguard around September of 2022, recieving the NPLQ Gen 9 along with training in first aid and CPR. As a lifeguard, I am expected to maintain concentration for long periods of time and ensure customer safety irregardless of the situation. Moreover I learnt customer service skills so that I can relay expectations in regards to safety in a calm and proffesional manner.I also learnt how to remain calm and effective in otherwise chaotic situations so that I can make the correct decisions.
As a first aider, my role is to reassure the customer that they will be okay and help calm them down whilst performing first aid of vaeying degrees of difficulty. This not only demonstrates my ability to multi task well, but further shows a high level of communication skills as I am able to clearly and effectively talk to a customer suffering from varying degrees of pain.
Outside of my role of ensuring customer safety and providing first aid, I am also expected to help maintain a high level of cleanliness across the building. This has helped me gain an eye for fine details and an ability to work in less than pleasant environments.

Rookie LifeGuard Instructor

I have been a Rookie lifeguard instructor since the 1st of February 2025. The role of a rookie lifeguard instructor primarily focuses on being able to deliver knowleedge on life seaving skills to a younger audience in a safe and effective manner whilst retaining their interest. As part of this role, I learnt how to communicate expectations and knowledge to children of varying ages and varying levels of interest. In addition to the increased communication skills i gained from this role, I also learned how to effectively focus on several tasks occuring simultaneously, as I am expected to teach a class of several children in varying locations.
This role has also given me more insight in how to think on my feet and adapt a plan to fit the current circumstances and still produce the desired result.

Rookie Rescue Instructor

As of the 25th of March 2025 I have become a qualified rookie rescue instructor. This course is primarily aimed at younger children with disabilities that means that they otherwise would be unable to learn the skills present in the Rookie lifeguard course. As a result of my training for this qualification I have learnt how to better teach children with disabilities and how to adapt pre set expectations to aid the learning of crucial life saving skills.

Swim Teaching

As of the 29th of March 2025, I have passed the practical and theoretical aspects of the STA award in teaching swimming. I am currently waiting for my portfolio to be checked, but after that I will be an STA qualified swim teacher .Through this course i have learnt how to better communicate my ideas to younger children, and have been able to learn more about how people learn best. Moreover as part of the course i have completed safeguarding training for vulnerable adults and children which has helped me learn how to look out for hidden signs.
In addition to this, I have gained more confidence in communicating with both children and parents on what will be covered during the lesson, as well as taking control on the pacing of the lesson in order to ensure all participants are listening.
